# 2024 UK General Election — Reform UK Target Seats (by gap to winner)

Every seat where Reform UK finished 2nd, 3rd or 4th, ranked by how far behind the winner it was (winner's votes − Reform's votes, as a percentage of valid votes), smallest gap first. Finishing position is shown but does not affect the ranking — a narrow third in a three-way split ranks above a distant second.

## Key findings

- Reform UK **won 5 seats**; this list covers the **544** further seats where it finished 2nd–4th.
- By finishing position: **98 second, 336 third, 110 fourth** — most of Reform's closest contests were ones it did *not* finish second in.
- By winning party: **Labour 349, Conservative 114**, others 81.
- By nation: **469 England, 31 Wales**, 44 Scotland.
- **5 seats within 5 pts** of the winner and **17 within 10 pts** — the most realistic gains.
- Median gap to winner: **23.8 pts** (mean 25.4 pts).

## Methodology

- **Winner−Reform** = winning candidate's votes − Reform UK's votes, shown both as a raw count and as a percentage of valid votes cast. Where Reform was the runner-up this equals the seat's official majority; where it finished 3rd or 4th it is the gap Reform would need to close to win, irrespective of who came second.
- **Reform pos** = Reform UK's finishing position in the seat.
- **Reform vote share** = Reform UK's votes as a percentage of valid votes cast.
- Seats are ranked by the percentage gap, smallest first; ties are broken on full-precision values. Finishing position does not affect the order.
- Scope: seats where Reform UK stood and finished 2nd, 3rd or 4th. The 5 seats it won, and the handful where it finished 5th or lower, are excluded.
- First/second/winning positions are derived directly from candidate vote counts.
