# 2024 UK General Election — Seats Where Reform UK Came Second

## Key findings

- Reform UK **won 5 seats** and came **second in 98** of 650 constituencies; in the remaining 547 it placed third or lower.
- Of the 98 second places, **Labour won 89** and the **Conservatives 9**.
- By nation: **85 in England, 13 in Wales**, none in Scotland or Northern Ireland.
- Median winning margin over Reform: **21.5 pts** (mean 23.8 pts) — in most of these seats Reform was a distant second, not a near-miss.
- Only **2 seats within 5 pts** and **6 within 10 pts**: the realistic set of plausible Reform gains.
- Reform averaged **22.4%** of the vote across these seats, peaking at 33.2% in Barnsley South.
- Beyond the second places, Reform finished **third or lower but within 15 points of the winner in 57 further seats** — competitive three-way splits (see table below).

## Methodology

- **Winning margin** = winner's votes − Reform UK's votes (the official constituency majority, since Reform was runner-up in every seat listed).
- **Margin (% pts)** = winning margin as a percentage of valid votes cast in the seat. This is the primary sort key, so the vote-count and percentage rankings differ where electorates vary in size.
- **Reform vote share** = Reform UK's votes as a percentage of valid votes cast.
- **Gap to winner** (three-way splits table only) = winner's votes − Reform UK's votes as a percentage of valid votes, used where Reform was third or lower so no majority over Reform exists. That table is ranked by this gap.
- Ranking ties are broken on full-precision percentages, so seats showing the same rounded value are still strictly ordered.
- First and second place are derived directly from candidate vote counts, not from any pre-computed position field.
